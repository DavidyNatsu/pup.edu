PUP Mulanay


PUP Mulanay serves as “The Avenue of Knowledge in the Countryside”.


The campus supports PUP's mission to democratize access to quality education to poor but deserving students of Bondoc Peninsula area of Quezon Province and the nearby provinces. It is located at Brgy. Sta. Rosa, Mulanay, Quezon, along the Bondoc Peninsula Hi-way, overlooking the Tayabas Bay and the island of Marinduque.


Since its establishment in 1993, it has produced graduates who are now employed in the Calabarzon area and the neighboring cities and provinces. To date, it hosts nearly a thousand students as the campus continuously forges linkages with public and private institutions and other entities to support its programs.



Message from the Director


Welcome to the Polytechnic University of the Philippines Mulanay Branch, the avenue of knowledge in the countryside.


PUP Mulanay serves as the conduit of the University to provide access to educational opportunities in the Bondoc Peninsula of Quezon Province which was once a depressed, disadvantaged, and underserved (DDU) area. Mulanay is on the threshold of development as an agri-based municipality and has the capability to become a trading center. Indeed, PUP Mulanay plays a vital role in educating the Filipino masses for rural development.


The opening of Open University (Pamantasang Bayan) in 1991 paved the establishment of PUP Mulanay in 1993 with the passage of RA 7645 or the General Appropriations Act of 1991 that provided Php5M capital outlay.


For nearly 20 years, it has continuously forged linkages with government and non-government organizations as well as private individuals and entitities to sustain its programs through scholarships and grants, infrastructure support, and extension services.


Be part of PUP Mulanay in pursuing your dreams and aspirations of uplifting your family through education. Join us in building the community and our country through genuine service to humanity!


Adelia R. Roadilla, DEM



History


The opening PUP Pamantasang Bayan in 1991 gave birth to the establishment of Mulanay Campus in 1993 through RA 7645 or the General Appropriations Act of 1991 which provided for a Php5M capital outlay. On April 2, 1991, the MOA between PUP and LGU was signed by PUP President, Dr. Nemesio E. Prudente, Congressman Bienvenido O. Marquez, Jr. and Mulanay Mayor Mariano A. Morales, Jr. witnessed by Dr. Ofelia M. Carague, Dr. Samuel M. Salvador, Atty. Honesto L. Cueva and the Municipal Mayors of Bondoc Peninsula.


Businessman-philanthropist, Mr. Francisco Q. Bocobo, President of F.Q.B.+7 Inc. donated a parcel of one-hectare land pending segragation to PUP represented by Dr. Zenaida A. Olonan, PUP President, signed on September 12, 1991. The pioneer director, Professor Angeles A. Morales with the support of Mulanay Mayor Prudencio M. Maxino, Jr. and Mr. Fidel N. Aguirre negotiated for an additional two-hectare donation from the F.Q.B.+7 Inc. for the campus expansion subscribed on July 16, 1996 which was formally turned over to PUP Mulanay through OIC-Director, Dr. Adelia R. Roadilla in November 2005. P. B. Dionisio & Co. also donated an additional five-hectare sprawling land through the initiative of Mayor Prudencio M. Maxino, Jr. and Dr. Fe R. Baronia who assumed as Director in 2004. Overall, PUP Mulanay owns eight-hectare land awaiting more development and expansion.


Initially in 1993 to 1998, the courses offered were Bachelor in Office Administration (BOA), Bachelor in Business Teacher Education (BBTE), Bachelor in Entrepreneurial Management (BEM), Bachelor in Secondary Education (BSE) major in English, Bachelor of Science in Accountancy (BSA), Basic Computer Technology (BCT) and Computer Secretarial (CS). In 1999, the Bachelor of Science in Agribusiness Management (BSAM) and the technical courses on Information and Communication Technology (ICT) and Office Management (OM) were offered in lieu of BSA.


Now, the campus provides four-year degree programs on Bachelor in Elementary Education (BEED), Bachelor of Science in Agribusiness Management (BSAM), Bachelor of Science in Entrepreneurship (BSE), and Bachelor of Science in Office Administration (BSOA) and three-year ladderized in Diploma in Office Management Technology (DOMT).


Since 2007, PUP Mulanay Branch, through the campus director, Dr. Adelia R. Roadilla, has continuously forged linkages with government and non-government organizations as well as private individuals and entitities to sustain its programs through scholarships and grants, infrastructure support, and extension services.




Vision


PUP: The National Polytechnic University



Mission


Ensuring inclusive and equitable education and promoting lifelong learning opportunities through a re-engineered polytechnic university by commiting to:


  • provide democratized access to educational opportunities fo the holistic developement of individuals with global perspective.
  • offer industry-oriented curricula that produce highly-skilled professionals with managerial and technical capabilities and a strong sense of public service for nation building.
  • embed culture of research and innovation.
  • continuously develop faculty and employees with the highest level of professionalism.
  • engage public and private institutions and other stakeholders for the attainment of social developement goal.
  • establish a strong presence and impact in the international academic community.



Goals


PUP Mulanay Quezon Branch is guided by the shared commitment of the whole PUP system to wards the attainment of the FOUR (4) GOALS of higher education namely;


QUALITY AND EXCELLENCE.

Provision of undergraduate education for global competitiveness

RELEVANCE AND RESPONSIVENESS.

Offering of courses that are relevant and responsive to the needs of the communitty where the branch is located.

ACCESS AND EQUITY.

Catering quality but affordable education to poor but deserving students of the area.

EFFICIENCY AND EFFECTIVENESS.

Optimizing social, institutional, and individual return from utilization of higher education resources.

Objectives


PUP Mulanay aims to:


  • contribute to the manpower developement of the Bondoc Peninsula area and the neighboring municipalities, provinces and cities;
  • produce graduates in agricultural entrepreneurship to utilize the resources in the area for prduction and developement;
  • supply the demands for basic and technical-vocational education; provide technical know-how in information and communication technology necessary in office adminsistration and management;
  • inculcate the branch core values of mentoring with passion for learning; unconditional and God-centered service; advocating transparency and accountability; nurturing collegiality; achieving excellence with integrity and creadibility and yearning for peace and developement.



PUP Philosophy


As a state university, the Polythecnic University of the Phillipines believes that education is an instrument for the development of the citizenry and the enhancement of nation-building and that meaningful growth and transformation of the county are best acheived in and atmosphere of brotherhood, peace, freedom, and nattionalist oriented education imbued with the spirit of humanist internationalism.
